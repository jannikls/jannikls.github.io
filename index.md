I am a [physicist](/theoretical-physics/) and investor. Since 2024, I invest at Founders Fund and live in Silicon Valley. I like to write [essays](/writing) and read obscure [books](/bookshelf/). I sometimes angel invest in friends' companies and like building lego sets of startups.

I left a theoretical physics degree at a young age, where my research focused on Galaxy Simulations and Star Formation. My path into physics was driven by an early obsession with programming and deep learning, but I ultimately left physics and academic research because I saw it as stagnant. I care deeply about increasing the number of scientific and technological breakthroughs and study the institutions that enabled them throughout history. I was born and raised in Hamburg, Germany.

Today, I help build and invest in technologies that matter. Beyond just investing, I think about how we can better fund frontier research and technology development -- from nanotech to other deep tech fields. I am especially interested in getting more young, ambitious people to work on these hard problems and designing better models of capital formation to back them.

I am grateful that my blog [The Schilling Point](https://www.theschillingpoint.com) was supported by [Emergent Ventures](https://www.mercatus.org/emergent-ventures) in 2023. I am currently reading about the end of the Roman Republic, the history of 20th century industrial capitalism, and merchant banking.
